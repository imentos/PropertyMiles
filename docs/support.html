📚 Frequently Asked Questions

How does automatic trip detection work?

Property Miles uses your device's GPS and motion sensors to automatically detect trips:

  • Trip Start: When you exceed 10 mph and the app detects driving or cycling motion
  • Trip End: After you've been stopped for 3 minutes
  • Background Tracking: Works even when the app is closed or your phone is locked

This means you never have to remember to start or stop tracking - it just works automatically!

Why does the app need "Always Allow" location permission?

"Always Allow" permission is required for automatic trip detection. With this permission:

  • The app can detect when you start driving without opening it
  • Trips continue tracking even when your phone is locked
  • You never miss a trip because you forgot to open the app

Privacy Note: All your location data stays on your device. We never send it to servers or share it with anyone. See our Privacy Policy for details.

How much battery does it use?

Property Miles is optimized for minimal battery impact:

  • Uses Apple's automotive navigation mode (battery efficient)
  • Only actively tracks when motion is detected
  • Typical battery usage: 2-5% per day with normal driving

Tip: If you're not driving (e.g., on vacation), toggle off "Auto-Track Trips" in Settings to save battery.

Can I edit or delete trips?

Yes! You have full control over your trips:

  • Edit: Tap any trip to add purpose, property, vehicle, or notes
  • Delete: Swipe left on a trip and tap "Delete"
  • Bulk Delete: Go to Settings and tap "Clear All Data" to start fresh
How do I export my mileage report?

Exporting is easy:

  1. Go to the Reports tab
  2. Select your date range (Month, Quarter, Year, or Custom)
  3. Tap "Export CSV"
  4. Choose how to share: email, AirDrop, save to Files, etc.

The CSV file includes all trip details formatted for tax filing and accountants.

What's the IRS mileage rate?

The app uses the current IRS standard mileage rate:

  • 2026: $0.67 per mile

This rate is updated annually. We'll update the app when the IRS announces new rates.

Can I use this for non-real estate purposes?

  • Rideshare drivers (Uber, Lyft)
  • Delivery drivers
  • Contractors and consultants
  • Medical professionals making house calls
  • Anyone with business driving expenses

How do I add a property or location?

To save locations for quick tagging:

  1. Go to the Locations tab
  2. Tap the + button
  3. Enter a nickname (e.g., "Office", "Staging Warehouse")
  4. Enter the address or use current location
  5. Save

Future trips near this location will automatically be tagged with the nickname!

Why didn't a trip get recorded?

If a trip wasn't recorded, check these common issues:

  • Location Permission: Must be set to "Always Allow" in iOS Settings → Privacy → Location Services → Property Miles
  • Auto-Track Disabled: Check that "Auto-Track Trips" is ON in the app's Settings tab
  • Speed Too Low: Trip starts only when speed exceeds 10 mph. Very slow neighborhood driving might not trigger.
  • Motion Not Detected: Takes a few hundred meters for motion sensors to detect you're in a vehicle
  • Trip Still In Progress: Wait 3 minutes after stopping for the trip to save automatically

Tip: Look for the blue "Current Trip" banner at the top of the Trips tab to see if tracking is active.

🚀 Getting Started Guide

Step 1: Grant Location Permission

When you first open the app, you'll be asked for location permission. Make sure to select "Always Allow" for automatic tracking to work.

Already missed the prompt? Go to iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → Property Miles → Select "Always"

Step 2: Enable Auto-Tracking

In the app's Settings tab, toggle on "Auto-Track Trips". A blue indicator will appear when tracking is active.

Step 3: Drive Normally

That's it! The app now works automatically:

  • Start driving > 10 mph → Trip starts automatically
  • Stop for 3 minutes → Trip saves automatically
  • No need to open the app before each drive

Step 4: Review and Tag Trips

After trips are recorded:

  1. Open the Trips tab
  2. Tap a trip to add details:
    • Purpose (showing, open house, etc.)
    • Property/location
    • Vehicle
    • Notes
  3. Trips are saved automatically

Step 5: Export for Taxes

When you need your mileage report:

  1. Go to Reports tab
  2. Select date range
  3. Tap "Export CSV"
  4. Share with your accountant or save for records

⚠️ Troubleshooting

Trips Not Detecting? Follow this checklist:

1. Check Location Permission

  1. Go to iOS Settings
  2. Tap Privacy & Security
  3. Tap Location Services
  4. Find Property Miles
  5. Ensure it's set to "Always" (not "While Using")

2. Check Auto-Tracking

  1. Open Property Miles app
  2. Go to Settings tab
  3. Ensure "Auto-Track Trips" toggle is ON (green)

3. Verify Speed Threshold

  • Trips only start when speed exceeds 10 mph
  • Very slow city driving might not trigger
  • Try driving for a few blocks to build up speed

4. Wait for Stop Detection

  • Trips don't save immediately when you stop
  • App waits 3 minutes to ensure you're actually parked (not just at a red light)
  • Check the Trips tab after 3+ minutes

5. Check Battery Settings

  • Low Power Mode can limit background location
  • Make sure Property Miles isn't in the Background App Refresh restricted list
